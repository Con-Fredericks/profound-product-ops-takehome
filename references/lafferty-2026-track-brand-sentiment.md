# How to Track Brand Sentiment in Answer Engines with Profound

Answer engines don't just mention brands in straightforward, neutral ways. AI responses often reflect positive or negative sentiment that can affect brand visibility. Tracking brand sentiment helps position a brand as authoritative and trustworthy, increases the odds of a positive audience perception, surfaces misinformation or outdated details, and helps spot negative trends before they cause churn or reputation damage.

## How Do You Track Brand Sentiment With Profound?

**Benchmark your brand sentiment.** Profound automatically calculates positive and negative sentiment based on the prompts a brand tracks, shown as a percentage with a daily breakdown, and split into positive (green) versus negative (red) sentiment.

**Understand themes in your brand sentiment.** Beneath the benchmark, Profound surfaces the themes that shape a brand's sentiment: recurring narratives AI references when describing the brand. Each theme shows total occurrences and overall sentiment direction. This list "can easily include thousands of themes." Rather than acting on all of them, the guidance is to focus on those that appear most frequently, using the occurrences column to prioritize.

**Assess positive and negative themes.** A dedicated tab lets a user drill into full AI responses driving a given theme, showing exactly what an answer engine says about a brand and what it emphasizes first versus later in the response.

**Identify trending sentiment themes.** A trending tab surfaces which sentiment themes are gaining the most occurrences right now, revealing which topics may be most urgent to address.

## Eight Ways to Improve How Answer Engines Reference a Brand

1. Get mentioned in the most-cited sources (via the citations dashboard, by citation category: social, earned media, owned, etc.)
2. Join relevant Reddit conversations (Reddit holds roughly a 3.11% citation share as of November 2025, per Profound's own research)
3. Build a YouTube channel (YouTube is the most-cited domain for Gemini and Google AI Overviews as of January 2026; per Profound research, Gemini and ChatGPT cite videos with 1,000-100,000 views most often, not viral videos)
4. Request updates to inaccurate third-party content
5. Refresh outdated owned content, prioritized by citation frequency
6. Produce new content addressing common negative themes, prioritized by occurrence frequency
7. Publish LLM-friendly (AEO-optimized) content: question-based headings, bottom-line-up-front structure, short skimmable paragraphs
8. Use query fanouts to identify the subqueries answer engines consider when synthesizing a response, to inform content coverage

Sentiment data can be fed directly into Profound's Agents to generate content briefs or drafts targeting underperforming narratives.
